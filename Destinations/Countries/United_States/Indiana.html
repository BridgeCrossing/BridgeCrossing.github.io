August 2020: I biked from Chicago's Buckingham Fountain all the way to Indiana Dunes National Park. Great experience. Was I tired?.....yes. Was it challenging?.....yes. But there was never a doubt in my mind that if I rented a well-maintained road bike, I could do it. Although the years in Chicago have been tough at times, moving there without any connections and trying to make friends, this is one of the few times in my life I can definitely say I'm glad I pushed myself to hang out with new people. This fellow Bay Area acquaintance and I started our journey around 9 AM CT brimming with excitement. However, due to multiple structural issues of my friend's old bike, we didn't make it to Indiana Dunes until night time. We considered ourselves quite lucky, with his bike breaking down just close enough to a bike repair home/shop that miraculously had just the right old parts that fit. Upon reaching our destination, we found ourselves on private property, but were able to quickly snap a few photos to commemorate our trip. I returned to the park a couple weeks later to properly explore the area, hiking up the steep sandy hills during a sunny day, breathing in the fresh lake air. While standing on top of a hill, I couldn't help but feel pride about our bike ride. Even though I always knew I could handle the physical toll, I would have never actually done it had I never met this fellow Bay Area acquaintance. He's definitely one of the more interesting people I've met in Chicago.
